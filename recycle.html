RECYCLE GO

Campus ARM × PackAge+ — Increasing Return Rates for Reusable E-commerce Packaging

Role
UX Researcher
Team
UX Researcher · UI Designer
Timeline
Mar. 2022 – Sept. 2022
Awards
Taiwan Sustainable Energy Innovation Competition

Project Overview

Recycle Go proposes integrating ARM recycling machines with PackAge+ reusable packaging systems across university campuses. By offering small digital incentives and making returns convenient, the project aims to raise return rates and reduce overall circulation costs.

Research Impact

  • Uncovered key behavioral barriers: 75% drop-off in packaging returns due to inconvenience and weak incentives
  • Identified market gaps: Through 5+ competitive analyses of circular packaging models
  • Data-driven insights: Synthesized findings from 10+ university student interviews

Projected System Impact

  • 2.4 increase in return rates: From 25% to projected 60%+ through ARM-PackAge+ integration
  • 40% waste reduction: Single-use packaging waste decrease on pilot campuses

Problem Statement

  • Taiwan’s high online-shopping volume generates massive packaging waste, while reusable-packaging return rates remain around 25%.
  • Insufficient incentives and limited, city-concentrated return locations reduce participation and create regional gaps.

Opportunity Statement

How might we use campus-based ARM and digital rewards to improve convenience and motivation for returning reusable packaging, expanding participation and stabilizing return volumes?

Solution

Focusing on university students, the system places ARM return machines on campus and connects them to the PackAge+ network. Students scan and return packaging at nearby stations to receive small monetary rewards or credits. Packages are then cleaned and reused via reverse logistics, creating a closed loop that increases return rates and lowers costs.

  • Target Users: University students (18–22)
  • Key Partners: EPA Taiwan, PackAge+, Universities
  • Key Value: Convenience + incentives → higher return rates & lower costs

System Flow

ARM × PackAge+ Integration — Campus return → Reverse logistics → Cleaning & reuse.
