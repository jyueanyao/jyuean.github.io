Thrive

AHA — Life's Essential 8: Move More · 2025

UX Research Interaction Design Healthcare

Project overview

Thrive is an interactive fitness & wellness app concept inspired by the American Heart Association's Life's Essential 8, focused on the "Move More" pillar. It encourages small, consistent activity through gamified challenges, simple tracking, and friendly social incentives — helping people build lifelong movement habits.

Core goals

  • Motivate users to reach 150 minutes of moderate activity per week via short, achievable goals
  • Make movement joyful by adding playful gamification and friendly characters
  • Provide personalized suggestions and reminders that adapt to user progress
  • Support social connection and gentle competition to sustain engagement

Key features

  • Daily activity tracker with quick-start actions (walk, dance, run, stretch)
  • Mini-challenges & streaks that unlock cosmetic rewards and new zones
  • Personalized goals based on current activity and preferences
  • Clear progress visualization and gentle nudges (timers, reminders, in-the-moment prompts)

Design & process

Research included clinician interviews, workflow mapping, and low-fidelity prototyping. We prioritized reducing cognitive load: shorter summaries, clear labels, and one-click access to source notes for verification.

My role

UX research, interaction design, prototyping, and stakeholder synthesis.

Outcome

Prototype sessions showed measurable efficiency gains in simulated chart reviews and positive qualitative feedback about clarity and trust-building UI patterns.
